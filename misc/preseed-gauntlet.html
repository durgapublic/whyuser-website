Part I: The Foundation

This is the most critical work. A brilliant pitch for a company with a weak foundation will collapse under the slightest pressure of due diligence. Your only job is to generate this evidence.

Founder-Market Fit: "Why You?"

Investors bet on founders, not ideas. You must prove you have an "earned secret"—a unique, non-obvious insight forged from deep experience.

Example "Earned Secret":

"We believe the **shareable artifact** is the new dashboard, because proof must be portable to win budget. Existing tools that trap insights behind a login are built for a world that no longer exists."

Problem Validation: The Pain is Real

Prove you're solving a "hair on fire" problem that customers are already trying to solve, however imperfectly.

The "Why Now?" Thesis

Articulate the tectonic shifts (technological, cultural, regulatory) that create a fleeting window of opportunity for your solution to win.

Part II: Building Evidence

Translate your foundational work into tangible, investable assets. The goal is to systematically replace every assumption in your pitch with indisputable, customer-generated proof.

Growth & Traction Velocity

VCs invest in lines, not dots. Your job is to show a steep, upward-sloping line of progress in your core metric, like LOIs acquired over time.

Demand & List Assets

Build tangible assets that prove there is a pull for your solution from the market before you have a product to sell.

Part III: Monetization & GTM

Show that your solution has real commercial potential and you have early signals of a repeatable engine for acquiring customers.

Part VI: The Mechanics of the Raise

With a strong foundation and evidence in hand, the final phase is about communication and execution. Run a disciplined process to create a competitive dynamic and close your round.

The Ask & Use of Funds

Calculate your ask to provide an 18-24 month runway to hit your seed-stage milestones. Be specific about how capital will be deployed to de-risk the business.
